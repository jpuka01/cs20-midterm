Navigating Boston: Our Tips on The Best Ways to Travel to and from Boston

If you’re looking to explore Boston but don't know how, there are several convenient options to help you make the journey. Here’s a rundown of the best ways to travel into Boston.

1. Public Transit (MBTA)

~ One of the most efficient ways to get into Boston is via the Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority (MBTA)

Subway (Red Line): Both Somerville and Cambridge are well-served by the Red Line. Major stations like Alewife and Kendall/MIT connect you directly to downtown Boston in just a few stops.

Buses: Several bus routes also link these cities to Boston, with the #47 and #67 being popular choices for commuters.

2. Biking

~ For those who enjoy a more active commute, biking is an excellent option

Bike Lanes and Paths: Both cities have extensive bike infrastructure, allowing you to easily cycle into Boston. The Charles River paths offer a scenic route with stunning views.

Bike Share Programs: Consider using Bluebikes, the regional bike-sharing program, with convenient stations throughout Boston, Somerville, and Cambridge.

3. Ridesharing

~ Rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are widely available and offer a hassle-free way to reach your destination

Convenience: This option is particularly great if you're traveling with luggage or in a group. Just request a ride and enjoy the journey without worrying about parking!

4. Walking

~ For those who live closer to the Boston border, walking can be a delightful way to travel

Short Distances: Areas like East Cambridge are close enough that a leisurely walk can be both enjoyable and practical.

Conclusion

With these options at your disposal, getting from Somerville or Cambridge into Boston is easy and accessible. Whether you prefer the convenience of public transit, the freedom of biking, or the comfort of ridesharing, you’ll find a mode of travel that suits your needs. So grab your bag and hit the road—Boston is waiting for you!
